About Us
Around 50 years ago, the editors of a magazine called Travel & Camera made a big leap forward, embracing not just photography, but the entire world. They'd cover food, wine, fashion, hotels, cruises, beaches, cities, hiking, skiing just about anything that anybody would do for fun. They'd need a new name — Travel & Leisure and a new look for that magazine. Much has changed since our first issue was published back in 1971 — for starters, we swapped the ampersand sign for a plus symbol and we launched this website in 1998. But one thing remained the same — and that's our passion for travel, both near and far. Today, we reach more than 16 million travelers every month, and our team includes a network of hundreds of writers and photographers across the globe, all providing a local eye on the best places to stay, eat, see, and explore.


The World's Most Beautiful Beaches

East Beaches

Whitehaven Beach

Whitehaven Beach is famous for its pristine white silica sand, which is 98% pure silica and feels incredibly soft underfoot. The sand is also so fine that it doesn't retain heat, making it comfortable to walk on even on hot days. Activities: Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, walking, and picnicking on the beach. The clear waters are also great for snorkeling and stand-up paddleboarding.

Sand Beach

The sun warms the soft sand as gentle waves lap at the shore. The air carries the scent of salt and the cries of seabirds overhead. The vast blue ocean stretches out to meet the endless sky, creating a scene of peace and tranquility.

Sunset Beach

A world-famous surfing spot on the North Shore, known for its powerful winter waves that attract experienced surfers from around the globe. In the summer, the waves are calmer and the beach is great for swimming, sunbathing, and tidepooling.

Elafonissi Beach

Elafonissi Beach is a beautiful beach located on the southwest coast of Crete, Greece. The beach is ideal for swimming and relaxing, especially for families with young children. The beach is surrounded by mountains and cedar trees, creating a picturesque setting.


West Beaches

Grace Bay Beach

This classic Caribbean beach boasts white sand, calm turquoise water, and luxurious resorts. It's perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and just relaxing under the palm trees.

Coronado Beach

Coronado Beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing. The beach is wide and sandy, with plenty of room to spread out. The waves are typically calm, making it a great place for families with young children. The beach is also known for its beautiful sunsets, which can be enjoyed from the many beachfront restaurants and hotels.

Cannon Beach

Cannon Beach is a small coastal city in northwest Oregon. It’s known for its long, sandy shore. Standing tall in the ocean, Haystack Rock is a seasonal haven for tufted puffins. On a headland to the north, trails in Ecola State Park offer sweeping views of the ocean, coves and a lighthouse. South is Arcadia Beach, with tide pools and a picnic area.

Carmel Beach

Carmel Beach is a popular spot for a reason. Its natural beauty and laid-back vibes make it a perfect place to unwind and soak up the California sunshine. Whether you're building sandcastles, taking a leisurely stroll, or simply relaxing on the shore, Carmel Beach has something for everyone.


North Beaches

Miramar Beach

Miramar Beach is known for its sugar-white sand beaches, turquoise water, and relaxed, family-friendly atmosphere. Popular activities in Miramar Beach include swimming, sunbathing, parasailing, snorkeling, fishing, golfing, and shopping. The beach itself has lifeguards on duty during peak season, restrooms, and showers. There are also many beachfront restaurants, hotels, and shops in the area.

Sinquerim Beach

It's located in North Goa, near the popular Candolim Beach. The beach is relatively small, but it's not usually crowded. There are several shacks and restaurants along the beach where you can get food and drinks. Lifeguards are on duty during peak season.

Candolim Beach

Popular activities at Candolim Beach include swimming, sunbathing, water sports, and relaxing on the beach. There are also several shacks and restaurants along the beach where you can get food and drinks. Candolim Beach is generally less crowded than some of the other beaches in North Goa, and it has a more relaxed and laid-back atmosphere. However, it can still get busy during peak season.

Glass Beach

The beach is famous for its smooth, colorful pebbles, ranging from white and brown to green and blue. These pebbles are actually pieces of glass that have been tumbled and polished by the ocean waves for decades.

South Beaches

Pink Sand Beach

Pink Sands Beach is a popular spot for a reason. Its unique pink sand and clear waters make it a beautiful place to relax and enjoy the Caribbean sunshine.Its soft, pink sand, which gets its unique hue from tiny fragments of red coral. Swimming, sunbathing, snorkeling, and exploring the nearby tide pools.

Hampton Beach

Hampton Beach is a popular beach resort known for its lively boardwalk, lined with souvenir shops, pizzerias, ice cream parlors, and arcades. Big-name touring bands and comedians perform at the Hampton Beach Casino Ballroom, and the Explore the Ocean World Oceanarium has biologist-led tours and a touch tank. On the southern tip of the barrier island, Hampton Beach State Park offers swimming, fishing, and campgrounds.

Folly Beach

Folly Beach is a city on Folly Island, in South Carolina, just south of Charleston. It’s home to Folly Beach Pier, stretching more than 1,000 feet into the ocean. Center Street is lined with surf and souvenir shops. Folly Beach County Park has picnic areas and a pelican rookery. As well as beaches and wildlife-rich habitats, Lighthouse Inlet Heritage Preserve has views of Morris Island Lighthouse, completed in 1876.
